System Settings & Communication

  • SSRs: Reggora will generate. If requested, direct the client to appraisalsupport@theloanstore.com.
  • Borrower Report Delivery: Reggora will send.. If a broker asks for a copy, direct them to appraisalsupport@theloanstore.com.
  • Borrower Email: Some orders may not include a borrower email. Use na@na.com to allow edits.
  • Fee Schedule: TLS uses Fastapp’s fee schedule. When changing product double check this sheet
  • ACE + PDR Orders: The UPD (bACE) connection has been established. ACE PDR orders via Asteroom are approved for this client.

 

 

STOP AND READ

Do NOT send payment links through the Fastapp system for this client.
All payment processing is handled through Reggora (the client’s integrated portal), even if specifically requested via comments or email.

 

Payment Protocol

  • ALL Payment is collected by the Reggora platform
  • Orders will sit in the clients system until payment has been collected and then it will send the order to us.
  • Proceed only if payment has been collected, confirmed via system messages from Reggora. Payment Confirmation
  • Upon receipt, confirm the fee matches the collected amount shown in the payment notification.
  • Funds transfer to Fastapp occurs automatically after key milestones:
    • Inspection Complete or Report Complete. 
    • Completion and Funds Transfer:
  • Refunds: Fastapp does not collect or refund payments.

 

RUSH Fees

  • If the order is marked “Rush,” we need to add the rush product  to the order. (it does not do it automatically – system limitations)
  • Verify that the collected fee matches what is displayed in Fastapp.
  • Use the “Expected Transfer Amount” or check the Appraisal Order Form (Supporting Docs) to confirm fee alignment.

 

Fee Modification Procedure

  1. Use Actions > Send Modification Request
  2. Check Send Fee Change Request and enter the new fee with a reason.
  3. Wait for client approval and fee collection confirmation (via system notification).
  4. Do not proceed until confirmation is received.
  5. If unsure, email appraisals@prmg.net to confirm that payment has been collected.

 

Due Date Modification

  • Modify due dates via Actions > Send Modification Request
  • Always include a brief explanation for the change.
  • Any delay (inspection or otherwise) must be formally approved in Reggora via modification request — not just verbal or email acknowledgment.

 

Cancellations

  • Wait for a system-generated cancellation message before cancelling in Fastapp.
  • Do not cancel based on comments or emails alone.
  • Early cancellation will cause integration errors.
  • Example confirmation message: “Order Cancelled” and “Refund Issued.” 
  • Example Here

 

 

 

When to Contact TLS

Notify or escalate to appraisalsupport@theloanstore.com when:

  • Inspection or delivery delays occur.
  • A cancellation request is received from a party other than TLS.
  • Contact information is invalid or unresponsive.
  • The appraiser has completed prior services on the subject within the last 3 years. (TLS must approve proceeding.)
  • The property has zoning, condition, H&B use, or external obsolescence issues.
  • The subject is listed for sale on a refinance.
  • The property is outside the appraiser’s geographical competency or 50 miles.
  • An appraisal form change is needed.
  • The property is unique (log, berm, floating home, off-grid, etc.) with limited comparable sales.
  • Current use is not highest and best use or has impermissible attributes.
  • Any address discrepancy exists.
  • There is commercial activity on the property.
  • Multi-family with ADU properties—must be escalated to TLS for guidance.

 

SLA Expectations

  • Review ETAs on rush orders and notify TLS same day if unable to meet due date.
  • Contact agents within 24 hours of appraiser acceptance (for purchases).
  • Contact borrowers within 48 hours of acceptance.
  • Even with lockboxes, confirm inspection scheduling with agents.
  • Enter inspection appointments in the system for TLS visibility.
  • Fee increases must be approved and reflected in the system prior to report delivery.
  • Reports must be delivered by the provided due date.
  • Revisions due within 48 hours.
  • ROVs due within 72 hours.

 

General Requirements

  • The assigned appraiser must perform the inspection and complete the report.
    Reassignment within the same office requires AMC approval.
  • Appraiser must disclose AMC fee and Appraiser fee in the report.
  • Appraiser certification requirements:
    • Certified Appraiser: Required for complex or >$1,000,000 transaction value.
    • State Licensed Appraiser: Allowed for non-complex 1–4 unit appraisals < $1,000,000 or complex < $250,000.

 

Commercial Appraisal Instructions

  • Full interior & exterior inspection required.
  • Property sketch must be included.
  • No third-party inspectors permitted.
  • General appraisers approved (MAI not required).
  • Form Requirements:
    • Loan > $750,000: 71A required.
    • Loan ≤ $750,000: 71A or 71B acceptable.
  • Note: TLS system only lists “Commercial” as product type, so product field may appear blank.
    Confirm required form (71A or 71B) and update in Fastapp system accordingly.

Client Engagement Instructions

Engagement Instructions

 

**If the property type is determined to be different than the product type ordered, STOP AND DO NOT PROCEED! Please advise the AMC asap!**

 

TLS ineligible properties include the following. STOP and Escalate to the AMC if a property is on the ineligible property list.

  • Condotels
  • On-Frame Modular Homes
  • Geodesic Homes
  • Single Wide Manufactured Homes
  • Boarding Houses
  • Bed and Breakfast Homes
  • Community Land Trusts
  • Homes built from unique construction including shipping containers, trailers, railroad cars, etc.
  • 2-4 unit properties w/Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU)
  • Properties not suitable for year-round occupancy
  • Properties located in the Coastal Barrier Resource System
  • Properties growing marijuana, regardless of allowance through state law.
  • C5 & C6 or Q6 rated properties
  • If any areas of the subject property were not accessible at the time of the appointment, the report should be made subject to inspection to verify condition, quality, and utility of the area.
  • Effective April 2 2022 - Appraisers will be required to use the ANSI® Z765-2021 (American National Standards Institute®) method for calculating square footage. If unable to adhere to ANSI method you must indicate “GXX001-“ at the beginning of the Additional Features field of the appraisal and provide an explanation indicating why you are unable to comply.

 

Appraiser should address the following items within the report:

  • Prior Services Statement
  • Exposure Time
  • Highest and Best Use Analysis
  • State Law Disclosures (as applicable)
  • Complete Legal Description
  • Confirm if property is non-arm’s length transaction

 

Market Conditions (MC) is required on High Value appraisals. The Market Conditions should be consistent in the report from the housing trends to the MC Form and the addendum. If time adjustments are applied in the market grid, they require explanation and market support.

 

Cost Approach: If site value exceeds 30% of the opinion of market value, the commentary should address if this is common and typical for the market and its impact on marketability. Vacant land sales should be included to support the opinion of value if readily available in the subject market.

 

COST APPROACH section must be completed with Remaining Economic Life included in all reports- suggestion.

 

Obsolescence: Functional, External, or Physical obsolescence should be reconciled and consistent throughout the report. Include supporting photos and analysis to support opinion and conclusions.

  • External Obsolescence: Requires a sale with similar obsolescence to demonstrate marketability.

 

Zoning:

  • The appraiser must clearly report Specific Zoning Classification and report/summarize the Zoning Description and allowable uses.
  • If property zoning is legal non-conforming, include the following:
    • Discuss if property can be rebuilt in its current density if destroyed. If not able to be rebuilt, discuss its effect on marketability and comparables (comps).
    • Site/source used to verify zoning. Provide contact information for the source.
    • Include one comp with similar zoning.
      • No Zoning – An explanation is required regarding whether there are deed restrictions in place and ensure comparables have similar deed restrictions.
      • Properties with Commercial or Agricultural zoning must have a highest and best use analysis. Include one comp with similar zoning.

 

If site size is over ten acres, discuss if the property is income producing or has excess land.

 

Multiple Parcels: Describe each parcel, including parcel number and legal description. Confirm the improvements for each parcel and complete a highest and best use analysis. Include the following details:

  • Impact on marketability
  • Lots are Contiguous
  • Confirm if all parcels have road frontage or are landlocked
  • Public or private utilities
  • Address excess or surplus land and marketability
  • Incorporate a survey or plat map
  • Describe all easements or encroachments and marketability

 

Private Street: Confirm if the private street impacts marketability and if a road agreement is in plane. Include details regarding the road agreement, including who maintains, costs, and provide comps with similar appeal and market impact.

 

Security bars: The appraiser must report when the property has security bars on bedroom windows or doors. Appraiser must also comment if quick releases are installed and if they meet city code. If bars are present but quick releases are not, the report should be made subject to their installation or removal of bars.

 

Age restricted neighborhood: Disclose if the property is in an age restricted neighborhood and provide comparable sales in the same or similar type communities to demonstrate marketability

 

Gross Living Area (GLA): Utilize the exterior building dimensions per floor to calculate the above-grade GLA of a property. Measure the below-grade area as well. Condo or Co-op projects should have interior dimensions to calculate GLA. Garages and basements, including those partially abovegrade should not be included in the above-grade room count. Unpermitted additions that change the footprint of the improvements may be given contributory value but may not be included in the GLA or room count. If the GLA differs from public records, discuss and provide explanation for variance.

 

Property Flips: Provide improvements and costs after the last transfer by seller.

 

Heat Source: If the subject’s heat source is alternative, discuss the source and provide a minimum of one comparable within the subject’s market to show market acceptability.

 

Pools: Empty pools are required to be covered or filled to ensure no health and safety issues are present. If not covered or filled, appraisal should be made ‘subject to.’

 

Properties should include, at a minimum, a fully functioning bathroom and kitchen. If not, report should be ‘subject to.’

 

If property is C5 or C6 report should be made subject to repairs or replacement. Grid should reflect C4 rating and report should list items to get to C4 rating.

 

Final value must be bracketed within the adjusted range of the comparable sales. If not, provide an explanation.

 

Solar Panels: If the property has solar panels, the appraiser must provide commentary on whether they are owned or leased by the borrower and if the property maintains access to electric utilities consistent with community standards.

  • Solar panels owned by the borrower: appraiser should consider in the opinion of value.
  • Solar Panels subject to a lease agreement or Power Purchase Agreement: appraiser must not consider in the opinion of value.

 

If deed restrictions exist, appraiser to provide the following:

  • Discuss the restriction and marketability
  • Analyze the deed in the addendum
  • Include comps with similar restrictions 
  • Confirm reconveyance or resale restrictions and if the deed survives foreclosure
  • Confirm transfer fees (if required)
  • Provide multiple opinions of value if relevant. For example, a deed restriction based upon income will have a formula in the deed. This formula should be disclosed, analyzed, and reconciled within the addendum to provide restricted value.

 

Reports must contain the following statement to be acceptable to FNMA: “This appraisal is made on the basis of a hypothetical condition that the property rights being appraised are without resale and other restrictions that are terminated automatically upon the latter of the foreclosure or the expiration of any applicable redemption period, or upon recordation of a deed-in-lieu of foreclosure.”

 

Plat Map: The appraiser to attach current tax record and a Plat map of the subject property. If a Plat map is unavailable, the appraiser should comment as to why the Plat map is not included.

 

Private Utilities (well/septic) - Appraiser must provide similar comparable sales that have the same private utilities as the subject.

 

Oil Tank - If the subject has an oil tank, appraisal must comment on its location and if any leaks or odors were noted.

 

Leasehold – If the subject property is secured by a leasehold estate or subject to the payment of ground rent, the following is required:

  • Appraiser must provide a thorough, clear and detailed narrative within an addendum that identifies the terms, restrictions and conditions regarding lease agreements or ground leases and any effect it has on the value and marketability.
  • Comparables with similar leasehold should be included.

 

States with Fee Itemization and Disclosure laws:

  • Appraiser to provide total compensation for the assignment and the AMC compensation within the addendum of the appraisal report.

 

New or Proposed Construction:

  • Include Model name, base price, and list/cost of upgrades.
  • Include at least one sale from within the subject’s development.
  • Include at least one sale from a competing development (outside the influence of the subject’s builder/developer).
  • Ensure all sales are verified by an independent source (i.e., MLS, HUD-1, Assessor, etc.).
  • All new construction comps must be disclosed within the report. If there are comparable sales in the neighborhood that have NOT been listed in the MLS (builder sales), TLS requests that the appraiser contact the builder’s sales office to obtain the required sales data or explain why this information was not available.
  • If the property has a second kitchen, comment if legally permitted and zoned. Report must demonstrate second kitchen is typical for the market through an analysis of at least two comparable sales also having a second kitchen. If illegal zoning/unpermitted, provide cost to cure to bring the subject back to legal/permitted status.
  • In the event the opinion of site value exceeds 30% of the opinion of market value, the commentary should expand to address if the site value is common and typical for the market and if/how it impacts marketability.
  • Vacant land sales should be included to support the opinion of value if readily available within the subject’s market.
  • TLS does not accept limiting liability statements that conflict with the limiting conditions within the appraisal form.

 

Comparables (comps):

  • While the appraiser may submit more than three comps to support the opinion of market value, three must be settled or closed sales and should be the first three comps on the grid.
  • The appraiser should use comps that have closed within the last twelve months. If the appraiser uses older comparable sales, the appraiser should provide data to justify and support the use in the report.
  • Include two active listings to support value opinion. If truly comparable listings are not available in the specific neighborhood, slightly dated or distant sales will be acceptable with adequate analysis regarding market or distance.
  • If the property is in a declining market, include at least one comp within the last 90 days.
  • Discuss the omission of nearby and recent sales. Discuss using aged or distant sales when closer sales exist in the subject’s immediate marketplace.

 

Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU):

  • Multiple ADUs are not eligible. Contact the AMC if a property has multiple ADUs.
  • Is the ADU legal per local zoning?
    • Is the property defined as a one-unit property with an ADU or a two to four-unit property based on the characteristic of the property? The appraiser must determine compliance with this definition as part of the analysis in the Highest and Best Use section of the appraisal.
    • Is it metered separately?
    • Does the unit have a unique postal address (different from the subject)?
    • Can the unit be legally rented?
    • GLA should be reported and adjusted on a separate line in the grid, UNLESS the ADU is contained within or part of the primary dwelling with interior access and above grade.
    • Report must demonstrate that the improvements are acceptable for the market. An aged, settled sale will qualify as a comparable, and an active listing or under contract sale will qualify as a supplemental exhibit to show marketability.

 

Is the ADU permitted?

  • Un-permitted: Appraiser must comment on the quality and appearance of the work and its impact, if any, on the market value of the subject.
  • At least one comparable sale with similar attribute to demonstrate marketability for un-permitted ADU is required.

 

Discuss the access between the main property and the ADU.

Provide photos of the ADU including any access to the main improvement and any direct access from the exterior of the subject.

Sketch should include all ingress/egress locations of the ADU.

Provide detail of whether ADU is completed in a workmanlike manner, including the condition. Include if common and typical for the market area.

 

Permitted/Un-permitted Additions:

  • Permitted: Appraiser to provide quality and condition of the work. Provide confirmation if building permit and/or another acceptable document was issued.
  • Un-permitted: Comment if completed in a workmanlike manner, including commentary on the conditions and quality of the work. GLA for the unpermitted addition should be reported on a separate line item, adjustments applied if applicable, rather than including in the Subject’s GLA. Discuss impact to value and marketability.
  • If the work was not completed in a workmanlike manner, it should not be included in GLA and the appraiser should provide a cost to cure to return the subject back to its original structure OR cost to cure to bring the addition up to a workmanlike manner, similar to the rest of the subject property.

 

Sketch & Photos:

  • Sketch should include all rooms, outbuildings, additional units.
  • Sketch must include all exterior perimeter dimensions.
  • New Construction – appraiser may rely on blueprints but must verify dimensions when completing the Completion Certificate.
  • Legend for GLA calculations must be included in report.
  • If the floor plan is atypical or functionally obsolete, thus limiting the market appeal for the property in comparison to competitive properties in the neighborhood, the floor plan sketch must include interior walls.
  • 2-4 Unit Properties: Sketch must include the rooms for each unit and the labeled entry. Sketch must identify living area for each unit and Gross Building Area (GBA). GBA includes all interior common areas such as stairways or hallways, based on exterior measurements of the building.

 

Photos Required:

  • Street View
  • Exterior
  • Interior (all rooms, living areas, garage, finished basements, and attics)
  • Updates/adverse conditions
  • Accessory units (interior and exterior photos, including all access points to the unit)
  • Amenities that have contributory value
  • Comparables
  • Aerial photo
  • Elevation/Side photos (FHA and MFH)
  • Windows with bars or safety latches that are wrought iron, including quick releases when present ? External obsolescence impacting the property
  • Required repairs
  • HUD Tag/Plate for Manufactured Homes
  • Photos of real property ? MLS photos (Exterior Only when accompanied by original photo from the street)

 

 

Commercial Appraisal Guidelines:

  • A full Interior & Exterior inspection of the property is required.
  • A property sketch must be included in the report.
  • Third-party inspectors are not permitted—you must personally complete the inspection.
  • Only an MAI-designated appraiser may complete this assignment.
  • Form Requirement Based on Loan Amount:
    • If the loan amount is over $750,000, the report must be completed on a 71A form.
    • If the loan amount is $750,000 or less, the report may be completed on either a 71A or 71B form.
  • An Environmental Addendum must be included in the report
